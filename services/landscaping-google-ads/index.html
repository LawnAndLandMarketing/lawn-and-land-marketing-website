Local Service Ads

Pay per lead, not per click. Google Guaranteed badge builds trust. Appears above standard search ads. Best for generating phone calls from high-intent searchers. We handle setup, verification, and dispute management.

Common Questions

What Landscaping Business Owners
Ask About Google Ads

How much should a landscaping company spend on Google Ads?

It depends on your market size, competition level, and revenue goals — but most landscaping companies we work with spend $1,500–$5,000/month on ad budget. In competitive metro markets, $3,000–$5,000 is often needed to compete effectively. We build a market analysis during onboarding that gives you a specific recommended budget for your area and target cost-per-lead.

What's the difference between Google Search Ads and Local Service Ads (LSA)?

Google Search Ads are traditional keyword-based PPC — you bid on terms like "landscaping company near me" and pay per click. Local Service Ads appear above Search Ads and you pay per lead, not per click. They also feature a Google Guarantee badge which significantly increases trust. Most of our clients run both for maximum coverage — LSAs for calls, Search Ads for web leads and broader targeting.

Can I run Google Ads without a good landing page?

You can — but you'll pay significantly more per lead and convert far fewer clicks. Google's Quality Score heavily factors in landing page relevance, which directly affects what you pay per click. More importantly, sending paid traffic to a slow, generic, or confusing page is burning money. We build conversion-optimized landing pages as part of our ad campaigns specifically to address this.

How long until I see leads from Google Ads?

Usually within the first week of launch. Sometimes the first day. Unlike SEO, Google Ads generates leads immediately once campaigns are live and approved by Google (typically 24–48 hours). The first 30–60 days are a learning and optimization phase — leads come in from day one, but cost-per-lead improves significantly as the campaign collects data.

Do I own the Google Ads account, or does your agency?

You own it. Always. Your Google Ads account is in your name, linked to your billing method, and you have full admin access at all times. We manage it on your behalf — we don't take ownership of your account. If you ever leave (though our 97% retention rate suggests you won't), you keep everything.
