Creative Formats

The Ad Formats That Win
for Landscaping & Lawn Care

Not all Meta ad formats perform equally for the green industry. We lean into what's proven to stop the scroll, build trust, and generate estimate requests — and we know this because we're running Meta Ads for dozens of landscaping companies right now.

B/A

Before & After

The highest-performing format in landscaping. A clear visual transformation — overgrown yard to polished landscape, cracked patio to new pavers — creates immediate desire. We structure these with strong hooks and a direct CTA: "Want results like this? Book a free estimate."

VID

Project Showcase Video

Short-form video (15–30 seconds) of a completed project — crew working, progress shots, final reveal. Video ads build trust fast and lower cost-per-lead significantly compared to static images. We script and storyboard these for maximum retention.

CAR

Carousel Ads

Swipeable cards showcasing multiple projects or services in one ad. Great for service variety companies showing their range — lawn care, landscaping, hardscape, irrigation all in one swipe-through creative. Higher engagement than single images.

RTG

Retargeting Creative

Specifically designed ads for warm audiences — people who've already seen your brand. These speak differently: social proof-heavy, review callouts, urgency-based seasonal offers. Retargeting closes the leads cold campaigns generate.

Meta Ads and Google Ads serve different roles in the customer journey. Run them together and you own the entire funnel — from "I didn't know I needed this" to "I need to call someone today." Most of our competitors run one or the other. We run both, coordinated.

Common Questions

What Landscaping Business Owners
Ask About Meta Ads

Do Meta Ads actually work for landscaping and lawn care companies?

Yes — when they're run correctly. Most landscaping companies fail with Meta because they run generic "call us today" ads to cold, broad audiences. The winning approach is visually compelling creative (before/after photos, project showcases, time-lapse videos) targeted at homeowners in specific zip codes who match your ideal client profile. We've generated hundreds of qualified estimate requests for green industry clients through Meta. The companies that say Meta "doesn't work" usually had weak creative or no funnel — not a platform problem.

How much should a landscaping company spend on Facebook Ads?

Most of our clients start seeing strong results at $1,000–$2,500/month in ad spend. Metro markets with higher competition may need $2,500–$5,000 to generate meaningful lead volume. The key metric we track is cost-per-lead — we target $15–$45 CPL for most landscaping services depending on average job value. We build a specific budget recommendation based on your service area, competition level, and revenue goals during onboarding.

Do you provide the ad creative or do I need to send you photos?

We handle creative strategy and design — but great Meta Ads for landscaping are fueled by great project photography. We'll tell you exactly what shots we need (before/afters, project closeups, team at work), and the more quality photos you can provide, the better your campaigns will perform. If you don't have good photos yet, we'll help you build a shot list and strategy. Some clients use phone photos, some hire photographers — both work if the visuals show real, quality work.

Can I run Meta Ads for my lawn care company if I'm not on Facebook myself?

Yes — you need a Facebook Business Page and Ad Account, but you don't need to be personally active on Facebook. We set up everything through Meta Business Manager, manage it entirely on your behalf, and you won't need to log in daily. That said, having an active Instagram or Facebook presence alongside paid ads significantly improves performance — social proof (reviews, followers, engaged posts) increases conversion rates on paid traffic.

How quickly do Meta Ads generate landscaping leads?

Faster than SEO, slightly slower than Google Ads for high-intent leads. Typically our clients see their first leads within 72 hours of campaign launch. The first 30–60 days are a learning phase where Meta's algorithm optimizes for your best-converting audience — leads come in from day one, but volume and quality improve steadily as the pixel learns. Spring campaign launches timed correctly can generate massive lead volume quickly.

Should I run Meta Ads instead of or in addition to Google Ads?

In addition to. Meta and Google serve different stages of the buying journey — Meta creates demand, Google captures it. Running both creates a full-funnel system where Meta builds brand awareness and desire, and Google closes homeowners when they're ready to book.
