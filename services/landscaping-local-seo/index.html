Common Questions

What Landscaping Business Owners
Ask About Local SEO

What is local SEO for landscaping companies?

Local SEO focuses on ranking your landscaping business in Google's local search results — specifically the Google Maps 3-Pack and localized organic results. When someone searches "landscaping company near me" or "lawn care [city name]," local SEO is what determines whether your business appears. It involves Google Business Profile optimization, local citation building, review management, and location-based content on your website.

How do I rank in the Google Maps 3-Pack for landscaping?

The 3-Pack is driven by three main factors: relevance (does your profile match the search query), distance (how close you are to the searcher), and prominence (your overall authority signals — reviews, citations, website strength). We optimize all three systematically: completing and maintaining your GBP profile, building consistent citations across directories, generating and managing reviews, and aligning your website's local SEO signals.

Can I rank in Google Maps for cities I don't have an office in?

Yes — through a combination of service area page content on your website and targeted local SEO signals. Google's service area business (SAB) feature allows you to define your coverage area without a physical location in every city. We use a specific strategy for expanding local rankings across your service area that doesn't require additional office locations — just smart content and consistent signals.

How important are reviews for local SEO rankings?

Very important. Review count, review velocity (how frequently you get new reviews), and star rating are all strong local ranking signals. But more than just rankings — reviews directly influence whether potential customers call you or a competitor. We build systematic review generation into your program so you consistently outpace competitors on this signal.

What's the difference between local SEO and regular SEO?

Regular SEO focuses on your website's rankings in Google's standard (organic) search results. Local SEO focuses specifically on the Google Maps 3-Pack and localized search results — which show a map, business listings, reviews, and distance information. Both are important: local SEO captures the "near me" and city-name searches that have the highest purchase intent; regular SEO captures the broader research and comparison searches.
