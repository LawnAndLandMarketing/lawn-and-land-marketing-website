Marketing Automation for Landscaping

Your Marketing Should Work
While You're Running Crews

You're running jobs all day. You can't manually follow up with every lead, request reviews from every customer, and nurture every prospect in your pipeline. Automation handles all of it — the right message, to the right person, at exactly the right time — without requiring your attention.

Research shows that responding to a lead within 5 minutes makes you 100x more likely to connect and qualify them compared to a 30-minute response. Most landscaping companies respond in hours — or not at all.

When you're on a job site, you can't stop to answer every inquiry. But the lead that came in at 2pm doesn't care that you're busy — they're going to call the next company that shows up in Google Maps. Automation solves this: the moment a lead submits a form or calls and leaves a voicemail, an automated sequence fires instantly — a text confirmation, an email with your services overview, and a link to book an estimate time. By the time you check your phone during a crew break, that lead is already scheduled. The game isn't who has the best marketing. It's who responds first.

What We Automate

Every Stage of the Customer Journey.
Automated and Optimized.

Automation isn't one thing — it's a system of workflows that covers every touchpoint from first inquiry to long-term retention. Here's what runs in the background for every L&L client.

▷

Instant Lead Response Sequences

This is the highest-impact automation in your entire marketing stack. The moment a lead submits a form on your website, Facebook, or Google — an automated sequence fires within 60 seconds. They receive a personalized text message acknowledging their request and confirming someone will be in touch. An email follows with your company overview, project gallery link, and a direct scheduling link to book an estimate. A task is created in your CRM for a human follow-up call. The lead lands in your pipeline automatically, tagged with the source, the service they requested, and their location. You wake up in the morning to a CRM full of pre-qualified, pre-warmed leads who already know who you are and have already been nudged to book. No more manually copying leads from your email to a spreadsheet. No more leads falling through the cracks because someone forgot to call.

Highest ROI automation
◎

Review Request Automation

After every completed job, the customer automatically receives a text or email asking for a review. The timing is optimized (typically 24–48 hours post-completion), the message is personalized with the job type, and the link goes directly to your Google Business Profile. Review volume typically doubles within 60 days of activating this workflow.

◈

Estimate Follow-Up Sequences

Sent an estimate and heard nothing back? An automated 5-touch follow-up sequence fires over 7 days — a mix of texts and emails asking if they have questions, offering to schedule a follow-up call, and addressing common objections. Close rates on estimates consistently improve after activating this sequence.

◉

Seasonal Re-Engagement Campaigns

Past customers who haven't booked in a while receive automated seasonal outreach — spring cleanup reminders, fall prep notices, winter treatment offers. These campaigns re-engage dormant customers at the exact right time without any manual effort.

◆

Job Completion & Upsell Workflows

After a job is marked complete, an automated workflow fires: satisfaction survey, review request, and an upsell message for a complementary service. A one-time lawn customer gets a maintenance plan offer. A maintenance customer gets a spring aeration add-on. Upsells that would otherwise never be offered happen automatically.

◐

No-Show & Cancellation Recovery

When an estimate no-shows, an automated message fires within 30 minutes offering to reschedule. Cancellation recovery sequences give customers multiple easy paths to rebook — recovering jobs that would otherwise be permanently lost.

◑

CRM Pipeline Automation

Every lead, estimate, and job moves automatically through pipeline stages based on triggers — form submitted, estimate sent, job scheduled, job complete. You always have a real-time picture of your business without manually updating anything.

✦

Referral Request Workflows

Happy customers are your best source of new business. An automated referral request fires 2 weeks after a successful job completion — asking customers to share your name with a neighbor and offering a small referral incentive. Word-of-mouth, systematized.

Common Questions

What Business Owners Ask
About Marketing Automation

What platform do you use for automation?

We use GoHighLevel, which we've customized and white-labeled as "Service Area Expert" — a CRM and automation platform built around the specific workflows landscaping companies need. It handles SMS, email, pipeline management, review requests, and appointment scheduling in one platform. You get full access to the dashboard, and we manage the workflows for you.

Does automation feel impersonal to customers?

Only if it's done badly. We write automated messages that feel personal — they reference the specific service requested, the customer's first name, and relevant details about their situation. Customers rarely know they're getting an automated message. What they experience is a fast, professional, attentive company that follows up every time. That perception of attentiveness is exactly what drives reviews and referrals.

Can I turn off specific automations?

Yes. You have full control over which workflows are active. If you're running a promotion and don't want the standard estimate follow-up to conflict with your promotional messaging, you can pause it. We manage the platform and can make changes quickly — typically within a few hours of a request.

Is automation included in your programs?

Yes. The full automation stack — lead response, review requests, estimate follow-ups, and seasonal campaigns — is built into our Service Area Expert program. The Green Accelerator program includes core automations (lead response and review requests). See what's included in each program →

What results should I expect from automation alone?

In isolation, the most impactful results come from the lead response sequence (higher lead-to-estimate conversion) and review automation (significantly more reviews, which improves GMB rankings and conversion rates). Most clients see their review count double within 60 days and lead-to-estimate conversion rates improve by 20–40% once instant response automation is active.
