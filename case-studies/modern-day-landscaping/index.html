The Story: Great Work, Inconsistent Pipeline

Modern Day Landscaping had built a solid reputation in their market. Existing clients loved them. Referrals came in regularly. But "regularly" wasn't the same as "reliably" — and inconsistent lead flow made it nearly impossible to plan for growth. Some months were packed. Others were quiet. There was no system, no predictability.

When the owners started looking at digital marketing options, they hit the same wall many green industry companies do: most agencies didn't understand the seasonal rhythms, the high-intent local search patterns, or the specific language that converts a homeowner into a landscaping estimate request. Generic marketing advice didn't translate.

Why the Growth Program Was the Right Fit

The Growth Program is L&L's growth-stage program — built for lawn care and landscaping companies doing solid revenue who need a complete digital foundation without the price tag of the enterprise-tier Service Area Expert. For Modern Day Landscaping, this was the right entry point: ambitious enough to want real growth, smart enough to want proof before committing to the top tier.

The program packages website design, local SEO, Google Business Profile management, lead automation, and monthly optimization calls into one integrated system. The key word is integrated — everything works together rather than as isolated tactics.

The Build: Month by Month

Month 1–2: Foundation First

The engagement started with the website — because everything else depends on it. A Google Ad that sends traffic to a slow, outdated site is burning money. A great GBP listing that links to a weak site loses the conversion. The website had to come first.

The new site was built around the market Modern Day Landscaping served: specific service pages for their highest-revenue services, location pages for the neighborhoods and cities they covered, clear calls to action above the fold, trust signals throughout, and mobile-first design that loaded fast on any device.

Month 2: Local SEO & GBP Takeover

Their Google Business Profile was like most landscaping companies': claimed but underoptimized. Wrong primary category, outdated photos, no service descriptions, and a 3.9-star rating that had stagnated for years. We rebuilt it from the ground up: correct categories, full service menu, optimized description with target keywords, photo refresh, and a systematic review request process that started generating reviews immediately.

Citation cleanup ran in parallel — inconsistent business name/address/phone data across 80+ local directories was suppressing Maps rankings. We standardized everything.

Month 2: Google Ads — Supplemental, Not Primary

Paid search was layered in as a supplement to the organic foundation, not a substitute for it. The strategy: target high-intent keywords in their exact service area with tight geo-targeting, strong negative keyword lists from day one, and conversion tracking wired to every form fill and phone call. Spend started conservatively with a clear optimization plan — no "set it and forget it."

Month 2: Automation — Stop Losing Leads

Before L&L, a new lead from the website sat in an inbox until someone had time to reply — which could be hours or days into a busy schedule. By that point, they'd already called three other landscaping companies. We installed a lead response automation: instant text and email confirmation on every form submission, personalized follow-up at 2 hours, 24 hours, and 72 hours. Response time went from unpredictable to instant.

The 4.8-Star Turnaround

One metric that doesn't get enough credit is the Google review rating — not just the count, but the trajectory. Modern Day Landscaping had 3.9 stars when we started. Not bad, but not compelling either. For a homeowner comparing two landscaping companies, 3.9 versus 4.7 isn't even a choice.

The review generation system we installed sent an automated request text to every completed job within 24 hours. It's polite, personalized, and timed for peak satisfaction. The response rate was strong from day one. Within six months, the rating had climbed to 4.8 stars with a significantly higher review count — enough to make any comparison a clear win.

Higher rating + more reviews compounded with better Maps rankings (reviews are a major local ranking signal) which generated more traffic which produced more jobs which produced more reviews. The flywheel runs itself.

What This Means for Your Business

The Growth Program story is the same every time: a company doing real work, underserved by their digital presence, who finally builds the system that matches their quality. The ceiling for these businesses isn't their service — it's their marketing infrastructure.

If Modern Day Landscaping's situation sounds familiar — good reputation, referral-dependent, inconsistent pipeline — the solution probably looks similar. The question is how much longer you want to leave that growth on the table.
