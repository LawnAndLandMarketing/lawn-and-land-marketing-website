The Starting Point: Good Company, Invisible Online

Artistic Landscape Features had been in business for years. Their work was exceptional — high-end hardscaping, custom patios, outdoor living spaces that homeowners showed off to their neighbors. Word-of-mouth kept them afloat. But word-of-mouth has a ceiling, and they were hitting it.

When prospective customers searched "landscaping company near me" or "patio installation NJ," Artistic Landscape Features barely showed up. Competitors with inferior work were ranking higher, getting the calls, and booking the jobs. The team knew the problem — they just didn't know how to fix it.

Before L&L
  • Outdated website that didn't convert
  • No consistent Google Maps presence
  • Zero paid advertising strategy
  • Leads went cold — no follow-up system
  • All growth depended on referrals
  • No way to measure marketing ROI
After L&L
  • Custom conversion-focused website
  • Top 3 Google Maps for target keywords
  • Active Google Ads campaigns
  • Automated lead follow-up sequences
  • Multiple traffic channels working together
  • Monthly reporting on every metric

Why They Chose Lawn & Land Marketing

Artistic Landscape Features had looked at other marketing agencies before. They'd heard the pitches — "we'll run your ads," or "we'll handle your SEO." The problem with those offers was they were single-channel. Throw money at Google Ads without a website that converts, and you're burning budget. Build a great site with no traffic strategy, and you've made a digital brochure nobody finds.

What made L&L different was the whole-system approach. Website, SEO, Google Ads, local maps, automation — all built to work together as one machine. And crucially: L&L works exclusively with green industry companies. Not home services broadly. Not "we'll figure it out." Specifically landscaping and lawn care. The difference in context was evident from the first call.

"They already knew how our industry works before we said a word. They weren't learning on our dime."

— Artistic Landscape Features, on first working with L&L

What We Built: The Full System

The engagement started with the Service Area Expert program — the full-stack option for established companies ready to scale. Here's what we built, in order:

1
Month 1–2
New Website — Built to Convert
Complete custom website designed for one purpose: turning visitors into estimate requests. Mobile-first, fast loading, service area pages for each major location, clear CTAs on every page, trust signals throughout. Live in 45 days from kickoff.
2
Month 1–3
Local SEO & Google Business Profile
Full GBP optimization: correct categories, service descriptions, photo cadence, review strategy, Q&A setup. Citation cleanup across 80+ local directories. On-page SEO for every service and location page. Tracked rankings for 25+ target keywords weekly.
3
Month 2
Google Ads — Immediate Lead Flow
Search campaigns targeting high-intent keywords ("hardscaping contractor NJ," "patio installation near me") with geo-targeting to their service area. Negative keyword lists built immediately to prevent wasted spend. Conversion tracking installed so every dollar of spend traces back to a form fill or call.
4
Month 2
Lead Follow-Up Automation
Before L&L, a new lead from the website would sit in an inbox until someone manually emailed back — sometimes hours later, sometimes never. We installed an automated response sequence: instant text + email on form submission, follow-up at 2 hours, 24 hours, and 3 days. Response rates went from inconsistent to systematic.
5
Ongoing
Review Generation & Reputation
Automated review request texts sent post-service. Google review count grew consistently month over month. Higher star rating + more reviews = better Google Maps rankings + higher trust with new visitors = more estimates booked. The flywheel effect in action.

The Results: 12 Months In

The full system took roughly 90 days to reach full deployment — website live in 45 days, ads running week two, automation built in month two, SEO compounding from day one. By month four, the results were measurable. By month 12, they were transformative.

Estimate Requests

The most important metric for any service business is inbound estimate requests from qualified prospects. Before L&L, Artistic Landscape Features was getting sporadic leads — mostly from referrals and the occasional Google searcher who found them by luck. After 12 months with the full system running, monthly estimate requests had grown by more than 3x.

Google Maps Visibility

Local SEO is a long game, but the trajectory was clear within 60 days. GBP categories, citation consistency, review velocity, and on-page signals all pushed their Maps ranking up. By month 6 they were consistently appearing in the top 3 results for their highest-value keywords across their service area.

Lead Quality, Not Just Volume

More leads matter less if they're the wrong leads. What the Google Ads campaigns and service area SEO did was attract homeowners with serious intent — people searching "custom patio installation" or "hardscape contractor" rather than general "landscaping." The estimate-to-close rate held strong because the leads were warmer and better matched from the start.

"The system runs. We don't have to chase leads anymore — they come in, get followed up with automatically, and our estimator books the consultation. That's what we paid for."

— Artistic Landscape Features

What Made It Work

Single-channel marketing rarely delivers compounding results. What made this engagement work was the integration — each piece of the system fed the others:

  • A high-converting website made Google Ads profitable (better Quality Scores, better landing page experience)
  • More Google reviews improved Maps rankings and increased trust on the website
  • Better Maps rankings drove free organic traffic that supplemented paid leads
  • Automation captured leads that would have fallen through the cracks and turned them into booked estimates
  • Monthly reporting showed exactly which channels were performing so budget could be allocated intelligently

None of these elements work as well in isolation. Together, they created a marketing machine that kept improving month over month — even without constant manual intervention.

Is This Your Story Too?

If you're a landscaping or lawn care company with exceptional work and inconsistent leads, the gap usually isn't your service quality — it's your digital infrastructure. Most companies in the green industry are leaving a significant amount of business on the table every month because their online presence doesn't reflect how good they actually are.
